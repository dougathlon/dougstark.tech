Cosmoludics treats play as a world-making problem. Reading C. L. R. James and Sylvia Wynter through cricket, ceremony, and games, it asks how play becomes a technique for ordering bodies, histories, and forms of life.

The pressure point is ambivalence. Games can carry colonial discipline while also becoming fugitive practices that remake who gets to count as human, player, critic, or citizen.
