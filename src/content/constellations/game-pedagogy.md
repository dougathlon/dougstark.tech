Game Pedagogy starts from a practical claim: games do not become teachable because someone says "play is learning." They become teachable when rooms, devices, libraries, prompts, and assessment routines make play available as shared evidence.

The work uses course design and game-lab infrastructure to turn play from a private preference into a method students can document, compare, and revise. Its public materials show pedagogy as an argument about access, attention, and institutional form.
