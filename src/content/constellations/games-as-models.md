Games as Models treats games as epistemic mediators. They do not simply package knowledge in attractive form; they arrange uncertainty so that players can reason, test, revise, and sometimes mistake action for understanding.

The project moves from gamification and mathematical game theory to 4D toys, Fluxus experiments, and art-science. Its recurring distinction is between using games to solve problems and using games to make problems thinkable.
