Gaming Habits treats play as a training apparatus. Games do not simply express habits; they produce them through repetition, awkwardness, difficulty, grace, automation, and the body's slow education.

The project follows that mechanism across QWOP, Farocki, Bateson, neoliberal problem-solving, David Sudnow, and graceful gaming. Its recurring question is how play changes what bodies know before thought has caught up.
