Preenactment names a cultural habit of training for futures before they arrive. It asks how operational games, simulations, and manuals turn uncertainty into rehearsal.

The central cases come through Harun Farocki, military and managerial simulations, and the ordinary forms of practice that make risk feel administrable. The question is not whether rehearsal predicts the future, but how it organizes conduct before anyone knows what will happen.
