Science Fiction tracks speculative media as a theory of practice: how bodies adapt, communities endure, and technologies reorganize the possible.

For now it remains private, but its public edge is visible in work on Octavia Butler's Parable novels. The broader problem is how speculative forms make survival, relation, and technological mediation available to thought.
