Simema begins with a blunt question: if a game plays itself, why are we so eager to call that posthuman agency rather than spectatorship?

The concept names a hybrid form: cinema plus simulation. It treats autoplay and self-playing games as moving images that still address us, asking how automation reorganizes attention, passivity, and the pleasure of watching systems behave.
