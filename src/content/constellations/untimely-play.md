Untimely Play begins with the dissertation, "Untimely Play: History, Habit, Games." It treats older and minor game criticism as live theory: not precedent to cite, but machinery for asking different questions now.

The project follows figures such as David Sudnow, George Brecht, and Lewis Carroll to show how historical cases unsettle the field's present habits. The wager is simple: neglected criticism can produce better problems than the canon we already know how to use.
