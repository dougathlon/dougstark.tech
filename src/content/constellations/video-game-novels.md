Video Game Novels asks what happens when fiction learns from games. These works do more than mention controllers or virtual worlds; they borrow game structures for training readers in agency, failure, progress, and self-optimization.

The project remains archival because the center of the research has moved, but the problem still matters: game culture reshapes literary form by making practice, error, and upgrade into narrative habits.
