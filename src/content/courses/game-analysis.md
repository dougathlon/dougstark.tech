## Catalog Description

Games make meaning by organizing attention: what players can see, hear, repeat, misunderstand, and learn to do. ENGL/CSE 4362 teaches close play as a method for turning those experiences into arguments.

Each unit pairs a design term with a playable case: perspective, rules, mechanics, loops, sound, game feel, time, worlding, and critical play. Students synthesize empirical observations with theory, then finish with either a video essay or a prototype shown in a small public-facing showcase.
