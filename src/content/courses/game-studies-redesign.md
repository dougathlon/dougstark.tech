## Catalog Description

Game Studies asks what changes when culture is organized through playable systems. The course begins with definitions of play and games, then follows the field toward formal analysis, representation, labor, esports, autoplay, environmental collapse, and platform capitalism.

Students pair readings with accessible games so theory has to answer to play. No prior devotion to games is required; the point is to understand how rules, interfaces, identities, economies, histories, and pleasures make conduct feel possible, valuable, difficult, or ordinary.
