## Catalog Description

This earlier course established the pathway into the current Game Studies sequence. It asked students to treat games as historical, formal, and cultural objects whose rules and interfaces organize action.

Students moved from definitions of play and games toward close playing, representation, material labor, autoplay, and ecogaming. The later courses sharpen that work into distinct tracks: field introduction, close analysis, esports, and public-facing game pedagogy.
