## Catalog Description

According to Max Horkheimer, whereas traditional theory attempts to explain and understand the world, critical theory strives to critique and change the world. This course surveys currents in twentieth- and twenty-first-century critical theory that, at the very least, changed the academic world.

The course begins by asking what critical theory is, how to read it, and why people are skeptical of it. It then charts a rough chronology from New Criticism through structuralism, psychoanalysis, Marxism, poststructuralism, postcolonialism, feminism, and queer theory to contemporary debates regarding posthumanism, postcritique, and artificial intelligence.

The course is conceptualized as role-play. Students take on the role of scholars, with assignments designed to exercise the habits of critical inquiry: regular reading notes, a 15-minute in-class debrief, an oral exam, and two review essays on recent research in the student's field.
