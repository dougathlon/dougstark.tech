## Catalog Description

Critical theory is not a list of difficult names. It is a way of asking how culture, language, economy, power, and embodiment arrange what can be thought and done.

The course begins with the practical problem of reading theory well, then moves from New Criticism through structuralism, psychoanalysis, Marxism, poststructuralism, feminism, queer theory, postcolonialism, posthumanism, digital humanities, and artificial intelligence.

The work is scholarly role-play. Students practice the habits of critical inquiry through reading notes, teaching or debrief sessions, review essays, and oral examination, leaving with a map of major traditions and a method for making theory answer to their own research problems.
