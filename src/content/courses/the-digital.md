## Catalog Description

The Digital begins with a formal distinction: digital media organize the world by making it discrete, countable, addressable, and repeatable. That does not make culture less embodied; it changes how embodiment is stored, circulated, and governed.

The semester moves through infrastructure, automation, simulation, AI, interface, virtual reality, surveillance, remix, glitch, and speculation. Students research a keyword, build a short video essay for public screening, and use the course to think with digital culture rather than only critique it from outside.
