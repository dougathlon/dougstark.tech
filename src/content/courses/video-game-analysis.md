## Catalog Description

Games make meaning by organizing attention: what players can see, hear, repeat, misunderstand, and learn to do. This earlier version of ENGL/CSE 4362 taught close play as a method for turning those experiences into arguments.

Each unit paired a design concept with a playable case: text games, perspective, rules, mechanics, sound, game feel, flow, time, narrative, emotion, and critical play. Students ended by turning analysis into either a video essay, a game, or a walkthrough with an artist's statement.
