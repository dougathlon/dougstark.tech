On Simema argues that self-playing games are not simply games with less agency. They are automated moving images that address viewers through simulation, making passivity a central aesthetic relation rather than a failure of play.
"On Simema: The Radical Passivity of Self-Playing Games" is a chapter in Game Engine Culture(s), edited by Matteo Bittanti, Paolo Ruffino, and Valentino Catricalà.
