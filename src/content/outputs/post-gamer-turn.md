"I'd Rather Be a Neologism than a Gamer" asks what becomes possible when game culture stops treating "gamer" as its default subject.

The chapter brings intersectionality and assemblage to the post-gamer turn, treating naming as an analytic problem: who gets gathered by a term, who gets displaced by it, and what forms of play become legible afterward.
