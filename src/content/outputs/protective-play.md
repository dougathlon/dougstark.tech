Protective Play studies rehearsal as a political form. In Farocki's operational games and training images, play teaches people to inhabit risk before the event arrives, making future conduct feel already practiced.
"Protective Play: Farocki's Operational Games" develops a theory of play as protection: a way to rehearse danger, train perception, and acquire habits without directly undergoing the risks of the real scenario.

The paper sits between Preenactment and Gaming Habits. It links military wargames, operational images, simulation, rehearsal, and training to the broader question of how games prepare bodies and institutions for futures they attempt to control.
