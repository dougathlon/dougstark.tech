"Simema: The Aesthetics of Autoplay in Freudenheim's Schema" studies a game-engine artwork that plays with and partly without us. The chapter argues that autoplay makes spectatorship active in a different register: attunement rather than control.
"Simema: The Aesthetics of Autoplay in Freudenheim's Schema" appeared in Interplay, edited by William Morgan and Will Freudenheim, Caldo Worldwide, 2024.
